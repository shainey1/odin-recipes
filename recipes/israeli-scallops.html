Israeli Couscous & Scallops

colorful medley of seasoned Israeli couscous tossed with sweet corn, halved cherry tomatoes, crisp spring onions, fresh cilantro, and a hint of jalapeño for gentle heat. Topped with golden-seared scallops, this dish is a vibrant balance of sweetness, spice, and herbaceous freshness—perfectly layered for a light yet satisfying bite.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup Israeli (Pearl) Couscous
  • 1 ear corn, kernels removed
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 3 tablespoosn olive oil, divided
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons thinly sliced scallions, white and light green only
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 jalapeno, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup roughly chopped cilantro
  • 6 scallops

Directions

  1. Add broth to skillet and bring to a boil.
  2. Add couscous, corn, salt and pepper. Stir to combine.
  3. Reduce heat to bare simmer and cover.
  4. Cook for 8 to minutes, stirring occasionally, until liquid is absorbed.
  5. Add 1 tbsp of oil and the lemon juice, stir, and cover and cook for an additional minute.
  6. Put couscous in a serving bowl and combine with scallions, tomatoes, jalapeno, and cilantro.
  7. Dry scallops and season generously with salt and pepper.
  8. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium-high heat until barely smoking.
  9. Add the scallops and cook until golden brown on first side, 2 to 3 minutes.
  10. Flip and cook an additional 2 minutes longer until barely cooked through.
  11. Place scallops on top of couscous and serve immediately.
