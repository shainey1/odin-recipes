Chocolate Chip Cookies

Chocolate chip cookies are classic, buttery cookies studded with rich, gooey chocolate chips. Crisp on the edges and soft and chewy in the center, they strike a perfect balance of sweet, salty, and indulgent. Freshly baked, their warm, comforting aroma fills the kitchen, making them a timeless treat for all ages.

Ingredients

  • 1 3/4 cups all purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 14 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 cup packed light brown sugar
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 egg
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips

Directions

  1. Pre-heat oven to 375 degrees F
  2. Line a baking sheet with baking paper or silicone mat
  3. Whisky flour and baking soda together in a small bowl and leave to the side
  4. Melt and 10 tbsps of butter over medium-high heat
  5. Once melted, cook while swirling until the butter is a dark brown color
  6. Remove butter from heat and pour into a mixing bowl with the remaining 4 tbsps of butter. Beat until all the butter is melted and combined.
  7. Add sugar, salt, vanilla, egg, and egg yolk to the melted butter and mix until smooth with no lumps.
  8. Wait 3 minutes, then whisk for 30 seconds. Repeat this process 2 more times.
  9. Gently mix in flour mix until just combined.
  10. Mix in chocolate chips.
  11. Combine about 3 tablespoons of dough into a ball and place seperated on the baking sheet.
  12. Bake cookies for 5 minutes, rotate tray, and cook for an additiona 5-7 minutes or until edged are lightly browned.
  13. Cool cookies on wire rack until firm enough to pick up.

Notes

  • Dough freezes well for a few months.
